## Market segmentation in Australia

### Segment definitions

Using widely used ABS/ASBFEO employment-size definitions:
- **Small businesses:** fewer than 20 employees citeturn8view0turn8view1  
- **Medium businesses:** 20–199 employees citeturn8view1  
- **Large businesses (enterprise):** 200+ employees citeturn8view1  

Many buyers will also talk about “mid-market.” Practically, that often maps to medium businesses (20–199) plus smaller enterprise divisions or business units that buy like medium businesses.

### Typical buyers, triggers, and realities by segment

**Small / SMB** buyers are often the owner, GM, or operations lead. Triggers are usually immediate pain: inefficiency, staff frustration, inconsistent customer experience, messy growth, or a tech purchase that is not landing. Xperia’s use of a scorecard/quiz and template-led support is aligned to this buying behaviour (fast diagnosis, fast clarity, low friction). citeturn35view0turn1view4

**Medium / mid-market** buyers are more likely to have functional heads (Ops, Customer, HR, IT, PMO). They will still buy on speed and pragmatism, but they start demanding clearer governance, training plans, stakeholder mapping, and KPIs, all of which Xperia already claims as part of its approach. citeturn1view1turn1view2

**Enterprise** buyers (including government and large corporates) typically require:
- stronger proof (case studies, references, measurable outcomes)
- governance and measurement
- risk controls (security, privacy, compliance)
- procurement fit (panels/standing offers, tendering systems, established onboarding)
